On Neutral Evolution & Mendel's Limits

Most allele frequency shifts you witnessed were not driven by selection — no allele is universally better than another. Blue embers tend to dominate by Act 3 not because they are superior, but because random chance amplifies small starting differences in small populations. This is genetic drift: neutral evolution through randomness alone.

Gregor Mendel's laws describe how traits are inherited, but say nothing about which traits should be inherited. Eugenics misread genetics as a guide for "improving" humanity — but diversity is the engine of resilience. Every allele you tried to eliminate may have carried hidden value in an environment you hadn't imagined yet.
Glossary
Allele
One variant of a gene. Each ember carries two — one from each parent.
Founder Effect
When a small group starts a new population, their random alleles shape all future diversity — not because they were better, but because chance selected them.
Genetic Drift
Random changes in allele frequency, strongest in small populations. Not selection — pure chance compounding over time.
Fixation
When one allele reaches 100% frequency. All diversity for that trait is permanently lost.
Bottleneck
A sharp population reduction that randomly wipes alleles regardless of usefulness. Survivors reflect luck, not fitness.
Recessive Allele
Hidden behind a dominant allele, but can reappear in offspring if both parents carry it. Cannot be "selected out" without eliminating all carriers.
Mutation
A new allele not present in either parent, arising from a copy error. The ultimate source of all new genetic variation.
Gene Flow
Movement of alleles between groups via migration and interbreeding. Isolation blocks it; barriers reduce it.
Neutral Evolution
Evolution driven by drift, not selection. Most genetic change across a population is neutral — neither helpful nor harmful.
